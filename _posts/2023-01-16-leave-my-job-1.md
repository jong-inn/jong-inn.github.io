### **About why I decided to leave my job**

> *At the very outset, I just want to record what I felt and thought during my career journy.*

<br>

I left my first job in July 2022. Actually, I decided it one and a half years before, but it was much harder than I thought. I was so sad that I couldn't keep working with my colleagues. They gave me advice whenever I asked and helped me when I was in trouble. I could feel a strong fellowship with them. The thought that I couldn't be with these people in the future was left until I made the last decision.

Most people think anyone can suddenly quit their job, but they seemed pretty shocked when I announced I was leaving. They thought I wouldn't quit my job for at least five years.

Before my resolution, I was on the same page. I really thought I could stay in this company for at least ten years. The company was nice. It kept its market share in second place. Also, it kept trying to lead the trend in digitalization. The compensation and perks were comparably great. Furthermore, organizational culture had been getting better. In Korea, it was usual to work until late at night without any reason. But, this company made a desktop-shutdown policy forcing employees to get off their work at 6 pm before the government enforced the law. It sounds very funny to people in other countries, but if they had not made that policy, employees would still suffer in hell. Anyway, many things proved that it was an excellent company to work for longer.

Then, why did I leave my job? In summary, I just wanted to find more exciting jobs. It could sound ridiculous to someone. And also, there are a ton of job-seekers who wish to join this company. But for me, I was getting not interested in it.

I really enjoyed my first year. Although my team's situation was very tough for the newcomer because we were doing a significant and unprecedented project, I learned a lot from my teammates, and working with them was amazing. We were building the in-house Natural Language Processing platform. There were no references for that kind of platform, and requirements for the system were vague, so we had to predict how this platform would be used. Anyway, my team went through all troubles and obstacles and finally accomplished the project. I could know what the one-team was, was really immersed in the work, and felt genuine happiness.

After the project, I wanted to be deeply involved in technical roles and responsible for various data-related projects. However, the company didn't allow me to take that position. The responsibilities were strictly divided into each role. It is this company's rule, and this phenomenon can often be seen in big companies. The technical field roles were taken to other outsourcing companies.

Furthermore, I realized I'm a person who wants more creative and challenging projects than I thought. I had believed I liked stable and static jobs, but I changed my mind. During the project, I told myself that I won't ever do this <s>shit</s> again, but I was addicted to the sense of achievement whenever I overcame difficulties. Reflecting on my college, every semester, I always put myself in front of the cliff because of my desire to achieve many things in each semester. However, I felt I couldn't pursue my goal in this company.

First of all, the government rigorously regulates the financial business. It is really hard to get into a new business with government permission. So, new projects are very limited. Second, the company's stance was also conservative. It was tough to persuade executives if the profit of a project was not that good. But now, I understand their point of view because the market of credit cards is already a red ocean, and the company has been making a steady profit. So, there is no reason to take a risk, and also, the company has to use a budget in the right situation.

Thus, I concluded that the current company was not perfect for me. Therefore, I had to move out to seek my goal. Also, it is an ideal time to convert into a new chapter in my life.
